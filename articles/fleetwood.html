FLEETWOOD MAC

My all-time favorite band is Fleetwood Mac. Every time I hear their music I get a certain feeling. One that I can’t necessarily define right away, but I would say it’s a mixture of comfort, inspiration and happiness. You have to move to their music, even if you’re just swaying. This band means so much to me. I have always listened to music from the sixties and seventies growing up, but my parents were not huge fans of Fleetwood. They know the hit songs and some things about them but it wasn’t enough for me to know as a kid. I found them when I was in middle school thanks to my best friends’ dad when he played Dreams. I put it on repeat and we just danced and sang. I instantly needed more from this band. Since then, I have always had them on my playlists and I continue to find new songs and develop new favorites. They even have a song called Caroline from their Tango in the Night album. A few of their songs you can never go wrong with include:
Sara, Never Going Back Again, Landslide, Gold Dust Women, Tusk, Everywhere, You Make Loving Fun, Little Lies, Seven Wonders, Don’t Stop, Song Bird, Second Hand News, I Don’t Want to Know, Big Love, Rhiannon, Say That You Love Me, Sisters of the Moon, Tusk, World Turning, Black Magic Woman, Over My Head, & Go Your Own Way

SONGS...SONGS...SONGS

All of these songs have a special place in my heart for evoking different feelings. The first is one that resonated with how I was feeling at a certain time in high school.
“Silver Springs” is a ballad that touches on unrequited love and friendship. (It’s one of their swaying songs) This song has made me cry on multiple occasions, it is definitely sad and about breaking up from this relationship. But I feel like this five-minute-long song can take you through different stages of emotion and you can come out of the other end feeling like you worked through something. One reason I think this song is so impactful is because its raw. Stevie wrote this about her bandmate Lindsey Buckingham, the two who have famously dated and broken up and continued to be in Fleetwood Mac together.
Favorite Lyrics: “so I begin not to love you, turn around and see me running. I say I loved you years ago, but tell myself you never loved me, no” as well as “time cast a spell on you, but you won’t forget me. I know I could’ve loved you but you would not let me. I follow you down until the sound of my voice will haunt you. You’ll never get away from the sound of the woman that love you.”

Silver Springs



Listening to Gypsy, you feel like a gypsy. It is such a fun song and I have done many interpretive dances to this one. I see this song as thinking about a different version of yourself and saying that that person is still within you now. It is reflective and makes you think about being free and just spinning around. The ending of this song is my favorite, there is a long guitar riff that repeats its melodic notes and it makes you want to spin. I wish this part of the song would never end I always, always hear it and have to replay the song so I can hear it again.
Favorite Lyrics: “I have no fear, I have only love"

Gypsy

 

Leather and Lace, this song is my mother’s favorite. Technically it is Stevie Nicks song with Don Henley of the Eagles but I’m including it regardless. This song is powerful yet sweet, it says that it is okay to be soft and it is okay to need your partner. It is the most beautiful love song because it is not just about loving someone a lot but it is actually about how this person is making you a better person. Stevie sang this song live with Harry Styles in 2017 and it is a beautiful rendition but nothing can top the original.
Favorite lyrics: “Is love so fragile and the heart so hollow, shatter with words, impossible to follow. You’re saying I’m fragile, I try not to be I search only for something I can’t see. I have my own life and I am stronger than you know. But I carry this feeling when you walked into my house that you won’t be walking out the door.” “Sometime I’m a strong man, sometimes cold and scared and sometimes I cry. But that time I saw you I knew with you to light my nights and somehow I could get by.”

Leather and Lace



The Chain is a classic Fleetwood rock song. It has hints of bluegrass twang mixed with ethereal mystery that so explain this band for me. This song starts out howling about the wind and leads to a full throttle rock out jam with all bandmates playing loud and powerfully. It is so hard to reach the end of the song and not thrash about. When this song was written, all the band members were facing relationships at their breaking point. You can feel the questioning of their lovers allegiance in this song.
Favorite lyrics: “Running in the shadows, damn your love, damn your lies.” “And if you don’t love me now, you will never love me again. I can still hear you saying you would never break the chain.”

The Chain



Bleed To Love Her is a song that I have just discovered. It came on when I shuffled music by Fleetwood on my Alexa device. From the first note I was hooked, yet another song by this band has grabbed my attention and held it. The song itself is very simple. It has a reoccurring rhythm and Lindsey Buckingham’s vocals at the lead. The song is comfortable and feels nostalgic and melancholy. The lyrics are quite sad explaining a relationship where the person has more love for their partner than they do. I can listen to this for hours and I still haven’t decided whether I prefer the studio or the live version better.
Favorite Lyrics: “Once again she steals away, then she reaches out to kiss me. And how she takes my breath away, pretending that she don’t miss me”

Bleed To Love Her
